# Task 09 – Momentum Comparison

## Problem Statement

Compare the momentum of:

1. A fly of mass $m_1 = 2 \text{ g}$ moving at velocity $v_1 = 10 \text{ m/s}$
2. A tennis ball of mass $m_2 = 60 \text{ g}$ moving at velocity $v_2 = 1 \text{ m/s}$

Determine which object has greater momentum.

## Theory

Momentum $p$ of an object is defined as:

$$
p = m v
$$

where:

* $m$ is the mass of the object in kilograms
* $v$ is the velocity in meters per second

Momentum is a vector quantity, but for comparison of magnitude, only the product of mass and speed is required.

## Step-by-Step Solution

**Step 1: Convert masses to kilograms**

* Fly:

$$
m_1 = 2 \text{ g} = 0.002 \text{ kg}
$$

* Tennis ball:

$$
m_2 = 60 \text{ g} = 0.06 \text{ kg}
$$

**Step 2: Calculate momentum of each object**

### Fly:

$$
p_1 = m_1 v_1
$$

$$
p_1 = 0.002 \times 10
$$

$$
p_1 = 0.02 \text{ kg m/s}
$$

### Tennis Ball:

$$
p_2 = m_2 v_2
$$

$$
p_2 = 0.06 \times 1
$$

$$
p_2 = 0.06 \text{ kg m/s}
$$

## Final Result

* Fly: $p_1 = 0.02 \text{ kg m/s}$
* Tennis ball: $p_2 = 0.06 \text{ kg m/s}$

**Greater momentum:** Tennis ball

## Interpretation

Although the fly moves faster, the tennis ball’s larger mass results in greater momentum. Momentum depends on both mass and velocity; here, the mass of the tennis ball dominates the comparison.
